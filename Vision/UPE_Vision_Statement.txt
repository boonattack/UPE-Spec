The Universal Painting Engine (UPE) is being designed as a complete, expert‑level painting system capable of generating professional, studio‑quality workflows for any miniature, terrain piece, or diorama. Its purpose is to give painters a tool that teaches with clarity, guides with precision, and accelerates learning without sacrificing depth.

UPE will produce full painting workflows and then expand them into detailed “deep dives” that explain not only how to perform each step, but why the step matters, what can go wrong, and how to correct it. These deep dives will include the level of instruction a skilled mentor would provide: brush sizes, stroke direction, paint ratios, consistency, drying times, environmental considerations, and practical troubleshooting.

The engine is intended to be AI‑agnostic. Any AI capable of reading the UPE specification should be able to run it simply by loading the document into a chat. This requires the design to account for platform limitations, upload caps, and safety filters — including those found in free AI models. UPE must remain portable, robust, and predictable across all platforms.

UPE exists to save painters time and to make high‑level techniques accessible. Instead of searching through countless tutorials, the user simply describes what they want to paint, selects a style, provides their paint list, and UPE handles the rest.

Every session begins with a single question:
“What do you want to paint today?”

From that moment, UPE will interpret the subject using a sophisticated recognition system. This includes optional photo analysis, a comprehensive Warhammer recognition module capable of identifying models by name, and the ability to interpret user‑described dioramas or scratch‑built terrain. When recognition is uncertain, UPE will confirm details before proceeding.

The Warhammer recognition system will go beyond identification. It will understand factions, heraldry, armour types, colour conventions, and material layouts. It will recognise multipart kits, pose variants, and sculpt differences, and it will generate workflows that apply consistently across those variations.

UPE will then determine the user’s tools — brush, airbrush, or both — and move into paint intake. The user will provide their paint list using a simple template, including primers, varnishes, oils, enamels, pigments, and other materials. UPE will interpret the list, resolve ambiguities, and present the paints back with full metadata (including RGB values) for confirmation.

The user may choose to restrict UPE to their list, allow substitutions, or let UPE fill in missing colours. They may also choose to work within a single brand, a mix of brands, or whatever UPE recommends.

Finally, the user selects a painting mode — realistic, cinematic, modern ’Eavy Metal, classic ’Eavy Metal, stylised, grimdark, or others. UPE will then generate a complete workflow, compact enough to fit platform limits but detailed enough to feel professional. After one final confirmation, UPE will produce the deep dives, section by section, with as much detail as the platform allows.

Paint lists persist for the session, and once the workflow is complete, UPE will ask whether the user wants to paint something else.

Above all, UPE should feel like the ultimate teacher — a patient, knowledgeable, expert guide capable of taking any painter, at any level, and giving them the confidence and clarity to create something they’re proud of.
